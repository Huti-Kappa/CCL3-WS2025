App Concept

The Use Case

Fast fashion has created a paradox: we own too much but wear too little. Liji solves "wardrobe inefficiency." By logging daily outfits, users visualize exactly which items are being neglected. The app calculates a real-time Sustainability Score and Cost Per Wear, incentivizing users to reduce textile waste.

Target User

Designed for the Conscious Fashionista and the Data-Driven Shopper. It is for university students and young professionals who want to know if their clothing investments were worth it and who wish to organize their cluttered closets digitally.

Usability

Test Plan

Hypothesis

We hypothesize that users will be able to complete all core tasks with a completion rate of at least 90%. Furthermore, we predict a System Usability Scale (SUS) score of >70, indicating 'Good' usability despite minor heuristic issues.

Data to Collect

  • Time on Task: Time taken to add a new item.
  • Completion Rate: Success/Failure per task.
  • SEQ: Rated 1-7 after each task.
  • SUS Score: Overall usability score.

Methods

  • Task-Based Usability Testing: See scenarios below.
  • Standardized Questionnaires: System Usability Scale (SUS).

Core Tasks & Scenarios

1. Create 1 Item & Find it

"Imagine you just bought a new shirt for €20. Take a photo of it (or use a placeholder) and add it to your closet with today's purchase date. Once you are done, find that new shirt in your main Closet list to make sure it saved."

2. Increase Wear Count & Edit Item

"You just wore your 'Green Shirt' again, so please increase its wear count by 1. Afterward, you realized you actually paid a different price for it. Find the Edit screen and change the price to €25."

3. Manage Outfits (Create, Edit, Delete)

"You are planning a look for tomorrow. Create a new collection named 'Date Night' and add two items to it. After creating it, decide that one item doesn't look right and remove it from the collection."
